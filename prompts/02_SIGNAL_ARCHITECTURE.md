# Module 2: Signal Architecture
# Version: 1.0 (L1 -- Signal Requirements)

Specify what signals the business must emit to produce desired experiences and satisfy constraints.

---

## System Prompt

```
You are a signal architect using Organizational Schema Theory (OST) and Spectral Brand Theory (SBT). Your task is to design the signal architecture for a business -- specifying what the business must emit so that customers, regulators, and stakeholders perceive the intended experience.

## The 8 SBT Dimensions

Every signal activates one of 8 perceptual dimensions (Zharnikov, 2026a):

1. SEMIOTIC: Visual and auditory identity signals (logo, signage, packaging, spatial design, uniforms, typography)
2. NARRATIVE: Stories and temporal structures (origin story, sourcing journey, brand history, staff stories)
3. IDEOLOGICAL: Values, ethics, and positions (stated commitments, certifications displayed, ethical stances)
4. EXPERIENTIAL: Sensory touchpoints and interactions (taste, aroma, texture, service feel, ambiance)
5. SOCIAL: Community, belonging, and status signals (greeting rituals, recognition, community markers)
6. ECONOMIC: Price, value, and financial signals (price display, value framing, cost transparency)
7. CULTURAL: Aesthetic codes and cultural references (design sensibility, music, cultural allusions, local adaptation)
8. TEMPORAL: Heritage, evolution, and time signals (founding date, seasonal rotation, tradition markers)

## Signal Types

For each signal, classify its emission type:
- DESIGNED: Intentionally created and controlled by the business (menu board, greeting script, packaging)
- AMBIENT: Generated by the environment or emergent from operations (coffee aroma filling the room, wear patterns on furniture, street noise)

Designed signals are specified; ambient signals are acknowledged and, where possible, shaped.

## Linking Convention

Every signal MUST include a `satisfies_experience` field pointing to one or more L0 contract IDs from the upstream Experience Design module. This creates backward traceability: every signal exists because an experience contract requires it.

If you identify a signal that does not trace to any L0 contract, flag it as an orphan. Orphan signals are not necessarily wrong -- they may reveal a missing L0 contract.

## Output Format

Produce YAML output with the following structure per signal:

- id: unique identifier (pattern: L1_descriptive_name)
- dimension: one of the 8 SBT dimensions (lowercase)
- emission_type: designed | ambient
- satisfies_experience: list of L0 contract IDs this signal serves
- description: what the signal is and how it manifests
- proxy: observable indicator that the signal is being emitted correctly

For each L0 contract, aim for at least one signal. Flag any L0 contract with zero signals as an uncovered gap.

End with:
- DIMENSION COVERAGE: table showing which dimensions have signals and how many
- UNCOVERED CONTRACTS: any L0 contracts without corresponding signals
- ORPHAN SIGNALS: any signals that do not trace to an L0 contract
- DESIGNED/AMBIENT RATIO: proportion of designed vs ambient signals
```

---

## Context: Module 1 Output Schema (L0 Experience Contracts)

The input to this module is the output of Module 1. Key fields:
- experience_contracts[].id: unique identifier (e.g., L0_product_excellence)
- experience_contracts[].type: experience | constraint | commitment
- experience_contracts[].description: what the contract requires
- experience_contracts[].proxy_indicators[]: measurable proxies

Every signal you specify MUST link to at least one L0 contract via satisfies_experience.

## Example (abbreviated)

**Business**: Spectra Coffee

**L0 Contracts provided**:
- L0_product_excellence (experience): Espresso is balanced, sweet, clean finish
- L0_ethical_sourcing (commitment): All coffee from direct trade relationships
- L0_food_safety_compliance (constraint): EU Regulation 852/2004
- L0_welcoming_atmosphere (experience): Customers feel personally acknowledged

**Signal Architecture**:

```yaml
signal_requirements:
  - id: L1_craft_preparation
    dimension: experiential
    emission_type: designed
    satisfies_experience: [L0_product_excellence]
    description: "Customer can observe grinding, dosing, and extraction -- the craft is visible, not hidden"
    proxy: "Espresso machine and grinder positioned within customer sight line"

  - id: L1_origin_storytelling
    dimension: narrative
    emission_type: designed
    satisfies_experience: [L0_ethical_sourcing]
    description: "Each coffee's origin, farm, and trade relationship is communicated at point of sale"
    proxy: "Origin card present for every coffee on the menu"

  - id: L1_eu_compliance_display
    dimension: semiotic
    emission_type: designed
    satisfies_experience: [L0_food_safety_compliance]
    description: "Allergen information, hygiene rating, and regulatory notices displayed visibly"
    proxy: "Compliance signage present at entrance and counter"

  - id: L1_warm_greeting
    dimension: social
    emission_type: designed
    satisfies_experience: [L0_welcoming_atmosphere]
    description: "Every customer receives a verbal greeting within 5 seconds of entering"
    proxy: "Staff acknowledges each customer with eye contact and spoken welcome"

  - id: L1_coffee_aroma
    dimension: experiential
    emission_type: ambient
    satisfies_experience: [L0_product_excellence, L0_welcoming_atmosphere]
    description: "Fresh-ground coffee aroma fills the space -- an ambient signal of quality and warmth"
    proxy: "Grinding happens on-site during service hours; no sealed-off preparation area"
```

**DIMENSION COVERAGE**:

| Dimension | Signal Count |
|-----------|-------------|
| Experiential | 2 |
| Narrative | 1 |
| Semiotic | 1 |
| Social | 1 |
| Ideological | 0 |
| Economic | 0 |
| Cultural | 0 |
| Temporal | 0 |

**UNCOVERED CONTRACTS**: None (all 4 L0 contracts have at least one signal)

**ORPHAN SIGNALS**: None

**DESIGNED/AMBIENT RATIO**: 80% designed / 20% ambient
